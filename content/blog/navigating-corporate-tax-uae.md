The UAE introduced its Federal Corporate Tax in June 2023, marking a new era for businesses operating in the region. At Majed Alshamsi Auditing, we've been closely monitoring these developments to ensure our clients stay ahead of their compliance obligations.

## Understanding the New Corporate Tax Regime

The UAE corporate tax applies at a standard rate of 9% on taxable income exceeding AED 375,000. This is one of the most competitive rates globally, reflecting the UAE's commitment to maintaining its position as a leading business hub.

> "The key to successful corporate tax compliance is early preparation. Businesses that proactively structure their operations will find the process smooth and manageable."

### Who Needs to Register?

All businesses operating in the UAE mainland are required to register for corporate tax, including:

- Free zone companies (with certain exemptions)
- UAE branches of foreign companies
- Sole proprietorships exceeding the income threshold
- Partnerships and other business structures

### Key Compliance Steps

1. Register with the Federal Tax Authority (FTA) through the EmaraTax portal
2. Maintain proper financial records in accordance with IFRS
3. File annual tax returns within the prescribed deadlines
4. Ensure transfer pricing documentation is in order
